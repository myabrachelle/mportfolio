Partner Reflection

Date: February 11, 2020

When starting this assignment, it was initially forced as we were simply following the prompts given on in the instructions. This created very concrete and short answers that did not help to better understand the other person. However, when talking about social identity and culture and their relationship with student leadership opportunities on campus, this opened up the possibility to have a deeper conversation. This allowed us to have conversations about family culture and their experience at the University of Michigan, overall. Over time, this assignment became easier as conversation topics were more free flowing and allowed us to learn more about one another. From this experience, I learned more about the importance of setting and structure of the conversation. As we initially entered this process knowing that a particular task needed to be accomplished, it limited our ability to create a space in which we could connect. However, after breaking those barriers, conversations were more casual. This connects back to previous experiences that I have learned how the environment can determine the overall outlook in the conversation. Typically most of these conversations occur during ice-breakers or at the beginning of the semester when I am trying to build connections within my classes. Despite this, I also have experience both in ResStaff and within my professional development as I have conducted many informal interviews that required the appropriate setting in order of creating that natural conversation. Without this, then the dialogue becomes very dry and ingenuine, not allowing the other individual to feel comfortable and safe to share their opinions.
This is important during my time on ResStaff as I need to learn how to use the spaces given to make residents feel more comfortable, which is crucial for my role. As someone who may need to have difficult conversations in my community, it is also important to establish the foundational understanding through initiating simple dialogue. Through this, it allows residents to recognize and understand that you are on a similar level to them and that there are similarities between the different individuals in the conversation. Moving forward, I hope to continue building on this skill, which may need to be more adaptive and creative when looking ahead to the upcoming election season. As different opinions may be shared across campuses, it is important, as a ResStaff member, to facilitate dialogue that allows for individuals to feel safe to share their values. Therefore, I must utilize the traits and skills emphasized during this activity in order of better preparing for changing climates and experiences that may occur in our communities.
